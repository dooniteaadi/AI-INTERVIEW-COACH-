## Getting Started

### What is AI Interview Coach?

AI Interview Coach is your personal interview practice partner. It helps you:

- **Practice** answering real interview questions
- **Receive** instant AI-powered feedback
- **Improve** your communication skills
- **Track** your progress over time
- **Build** confidence for real interviews

### Before You Begin

Make sure you have:

1. ✅ The application running (both backend and frontend)
2. ✅ A modern web browser (Chrome or Edge recommended)
3. ✅ A quiet environment for practice
4. ✅ (Optional) A microphone for voice input

### Accessing the Application

1. Open your web browser
2. Navigate to: `http://localhost:3000`
3. You should see the landing page with a role selector

---

## Your First Interview Session

### Step 1: Choose Your Role

On the landing page, you'll see a dropdown menu with various job roles:

- **Software Engineer** - Technical programming questions
- **Data Scientist** - Data analysis and ML questions
- **Product Manager** - Product strategy and management
- **UX Designer** - Design thinking and user experience
- **DevOps Engineer** - Infrastructure and deployment
- **Marketing Manager** - Marketing strategy and campaigns

**Select the role** that matches your target position.

---

### Step 2: Start Your Interview

Click the **"Start Interview Practice"** button.

You'll be taken to the interview page where you'll see:
- Your first question
- A text input area
- Voice recording button
- Interviewer mode selector
- Submit button

---

### Step 3: Answer the Question

You have two options:

#### Option A: Type Your Answer

1. Read the question carefully
2. Think about your response
3. Type your answer in the text area
4. Take your time - there's no time limit

#### Option B: Use Voice Input

1. Click the microphone button 🎤
2. Allow microphone permissions if prompted
3. Speak your answer clearly
4. Click the button again to stop recording
5. Review the transcribed text
6. Edit if needed

---

### Step 4: Select Interviewer Mode

Choose how you want to be evaluated:

- **Friendly** 😊 - Encouraging feedback, great for beginners
- **Technical** 🔧 - Focus on technical accuracy
- **Challenging** 💪 - Critical feedback for advanced practice

---

### Step 5: Submit and Get Feedback

Click **"Submit Response"** and wait a few seconds.

You'll receive:
- An overall score (1-10)
- Detailed feedback summary
- Tone analysis
- Clarity and confidence ratings
- Filler word count
- Specific improvement tips

---

### Step 6: Continue or Finish

- Click **"Next Question"** to continue
- Repeat steps 3-5 for each question
- After all questions, you'll see your final report

---

## Understanding Your Feedback

### Score (1-10)

Your response is rated on a scale of 1 to 10:

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 9-10 | Excellent - Outstanding answer |
| 7-8 | Good - Strong answer with minor improvements |
| 5-6 | Moderate - Acceptable but needs work |
| 3-4 | Needs Improvement - Significant gaps |
| 1-2 | Poor - Major revision needed |

---

### Tone Analysis

Your speaking tone is classified as:

- **Confident** ✅ - Strong, assured delivery
- **Neutral** ⚪ - Balanced, neither confident nor uncertain
- **Uncertain** ⚠️ - Hesitant or unsure delivery

**Tip:** Aim for confident tone by speaking clearly and avoiding excessive hedging words.

---

### Clarity Rating

How clear and understandable your response is:

- **Excellent** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Crystal clear, well-structured
- **Good** ⭐⭐⭐ - Clear with minor issues
- **Moderate** ⭐⭐ - Somewhat clear but could be better
- **Needs Improvement** ⭐ - Unclear or confusing

**Tip:** Structure your answers with a clear beginning, middle, and end.

---

### Confidence Level

How confident you appear in your response:

- **High** 💪 - Very confident and assured
- **Moderate** 👍 - Reasonably confident
- **Low** 😟 - Appears uncertain or hesitant

**Tip:** Even if unsure, speak with conviction and avoid excessive qualifiers.

---

### Filler Words Count

The system detects common filler words:

- um, uh, like
- you know, I mean
- actually, basically, literally
- sort of, kind of

**What's a good count?**
- 0-2 filler words: Excellent
- 3-5 filler words: Good
- 6-10 filler words: Moderate
- 10+ filler words: Needs improvement

**Tip:** Practice pausing instead of using filler words.

---

### Improvement Tips

You'll receive 3-5 specific suggestions, such as:

- "Provide concrete examples from your experience"
- "Structure your answer using the STAR method"
- "Reduce use of filler words"
- "Be more specific about technologies used"
- "Explain the impact of your work"

**Take these seriously** - they're tailored to your specific response!

## Using Voice Input

### Browser Compatibility

Voice input works best on:
- ✅ Google Chrome (recommended)
- ✅ Microsoft Edge
- ✅ Safari
- ❌ Firefox (limited support)

---

### How to Use Voice Input

1. **Click the microphone button** 🎤
   
2. **Allow permissions** when prompted
   - Your browser will ask for microphone access
   - Click "Allow" or "Yes"

3. **Start speaking**
   - Speak clearly and at a normal pace
   - The button will show a recording indicator

4. **Stop recording**
   - Click the button again when finished
   - Your speech will be transcribed to text

5. **Review and edit**
   - Check the transcribed text
   - Make any necessary corrections
   - Submit when ready

---

### Voice Input Tips

✅ **Do:**
- Speak clearly and at a moderate pace
- Use a quiet environment
- Position yourself close to the microphone
- Pause briefly between sentences

❌ **Don't:**
- Speak too fast or too slow
- Use voice input in noisy environments
- Mumble or speak unclearly
- Forget to review the transcription

---

### Troubleshooting Voice Input

**Problem:** Microphone button doesn't work

**Solutions:**
- Check browser compatibility
- Allow microphone permissions
- Try refreshing the page
- Check if another app is using the microphone

**Problem:** Transcription is inaccurate

**Solutions:**
- Speak more clearly
- Reduce background noise
- Use a better microphone
- Edit the text manually after transcription

## Resume Analysis Feature

### What It Does

Upload your resume and the AI will:

1. **Extract** text from your PDF resume
2. **Analyze** your experience and skills
3. **Generate** custom interview questions based on YOUR resume
4. **Roast** your resume (optional) - humorous but constructive feedback

---

### How to Use It

1. **Prepare your resume**
   - Must be in PDF format
   - Should be up-to-date
   - Include relevant experience and skills

2. **Upload your resume**
   - Click the upload button
   - Select your PDF file
   - Choose your target role

3. **Enable Roast Mode** (optional)
   - Toggle this for humorous feedback
   - Get called out on buzzwords and clichés
   - Receive constructive criticism

4. **Review the results**
   - See extracted text preview
   - Get custom interview questions
   - Read the roast (if enabled)

5. **Practice with custom questions**
   - Use the generated questions for your interview
   - These are specific to YOUR experience
   - More relevant than generic questions

---

### Resume Upload Tips

✅ **Best Practices:**
- Use a clean, well-formatted PDF
- Include specific achievements
- Mention technologies and tools
- Quantify your impact (numbers, percentages)

❌ **Avoid:**
- Scanned images (text must be selectable)
- Password-protected PDFs
- Overly complex formatting
- Generic buzzwords without substance

---

### Understanding the Roast

The resume roast is designed to be:

- **Funny** - Humorous and entertaining
- **Honest** - Points out real issues
- **Constructive** - Provides actionable advice
- **Motivating** - Pushes you to improve

**Don't take it personally!** It's meant to help you see your resume from a critical perspective.

## Interviewer Modes Explained

### Friendly Mode 😊

**Best for:**
- Beginners
- Building confidence
- First-time practice
- Overcoming interview anxiety

**Characteristics:**
- Encouraging and supportive
- Focuses on strengths
- Gentle improvement suggestions
- Positive reinforcement

**Example Feedback:**
> "Great job! You demonstrated a solid understanding of the topic. Your explanation was clear and well-structured. To make it even better, consider adding a specific example from your experience."

---

### Technical Mode 🔧

**Best for:**
- Technical roles
- Experienced professionals
- Assessing technical depth
- Preparing for technical interviews

**Characteristics:**
- Focuses on technical accuracy
- Evaluates depth of knowledge
- Checks for specific details
- Assesses problem-solving approach

**Example Feedback:**
> "Your answer covers the basics correctly. However, you didn't mention important considerations like time complexity (O(n log n)) or space trade-offs. In a real interview, be prepared to discuss edge cases and optimization strategies."

---

### Challenging Mode 💪

**Best for:**
- Advanced preparation
- Senior positions
- Stress testing
- Final interview prep

**Characteristics:**
- More critical and demanding
- Highlights significant gaps
- Pushes for excellence
- Simulates tough interviewers

**Example Feedback:**
> "Your response lacks depth and specificity. You mentioned 'working with a team' but provided no concrete examples. The interviewer would likely probe deeper here. You need to demonstrate actual impact with metrics and explain your specific contributions, not just team achievements."

---

### Choosing the Right Mode

**Start with Friendly** if you're:
- New to interview practice
- Building confidence
- Learning the basics

**Move to Technical** when you:
- Feel comfortable with basics
- Want detailed technical feedback
- Are preparing for technical roles

**Use Challenging** when you:
- Are in final interview prep
- Want to stress-test yourself
- Are applying for senior positions
- Need to identify weak spots

**Pro Tip:** Progress through all three modes for comprehensive preparation!

## Reading Your Performance Report

After completing all questions, you'll see a comprehensive report.

### Report Sections

#### 1. Overall Statistics

```
Average Score: 7.6/10
Overall Tone: Confident
Total Filler Words: 8
Questions Answered: 5/5
```

**What to look for:**
- Average score above 7 is good
- Consistent tone across answers
- Low filler word count
- All questions completed

---

#### 2. Score Breakdown

```
Minimum Score: 6
Maximum Score: 9
Average Score: 7.6
```

**Analysis:**
- **High variance** (big difference between min/max) = inconsistent performance
- **Low variance** (small difference) = consistent performance
- **Improving trend** = getting better as you go
- **Declining trend** = fatigue or difficulty increase

---

#### 3. Question-by-Question Breakdown

For each question, you'll see:
- The question
- Your response
- Score received
- Detailed feedback
- Improvement tips

**Use this to:**
- Identify your strongest answers
- Find patterns in weak areas
- Learn from specific feedback
- Plan targeted improvement

---

#### 4. Top Improvement Tips

The report highlights your top 5 improvement areas:

1. Provide more specific examples
2. Reduce use of filler words
3. Structure answers using STAR method
4. Quantify your achievements
5. Explain technical concepts more clearly

**Action Items:**
- Focus on these areas in your next practice
- Research techniques for each tip
- Practice specifically addressing these points

## Tips for Better Results

### Before Your Practice Session

1. **Choose a quiet environment**
   - Minimize distractions
   - Reduce background noise
   - Ensure good internet connection

2. **Prepare mentally**
   - Review the job description
   - Think about your experiences
   - Have examples ready

3. **Set up properly**
   - Test your microphone (if using voice)
   - Have a comfortable seating position
   - Keep water nearby

---

### During Your Practice

1. **Take your time**
   - There's no time limit
   - Think before you speak/type
   - Structure your thoughts

2. **Be specific**
   - Use concrete examples
   - Mention specific technologies
   - Quantify your achievements

3. **Use the STAR method**
   - **S**ituation - Set the context
   - **T**ask - Explain the challenge
   - **A**ction - Describe what you did
   - **R**esult - Share the outcome

4. **Avoid filler words**
   - Pause instead of saying "um"
   - Think before speaking
   - Practice mindful speech

5. **Stay confident**
   - Speak with conviction
   - Avoid excessive hedging ("maybe", "I think")
   - Own your achievements

---

### After Your Practice

1. **Review your feedback carefully**
   - Read all improvement tips
   - Identify patterns
   - Note recurring issues

2. **Practice specific areas**
   - Focus on weak points
   - Research better approaches
   - Practice improved answers

3. **Track your progress**
   - Save your reports
   - Compare scores over time
   - Celebrate improvements

4. **Iterate and improve**
   - Practice regularly
   - Try different modes
   - Challenge yourself

## Frequently Asked Questions

### General Questions

**Q: How many questions should I practice?**

A: Start with 5 questions per session. As you improve, increase to 10-15 questions.

---

**Q: How often should I practice?**

A: Ideally, practice 2-3 times per week. Consistency is more important than frequency.

---

**Q: Can I practice the same questions multiple times?**

A: Yes! Practicing the same questions helps you refine your answers and track improvement.

---

**Q: Is my data saved?**

A: Currently, sessions are stored in-memory and cleared when the server restarts. Export your reports as PDFs to keep records.

---

### Technical Questions

**Q: Why isn't voice input working?**

A: 
- Ensure you're using Chrome or Edge
- Check microphone permissions
- Try refreshing the page
- Verify your microphone is working in other apps

---

**Q: The AI feedback seems generic. Why?**

A: 
- Provide more detailed responses
- Include specific examples
- Use technical terminology
- Give the AI more content to analyze

---

**Q: Can I use this on mobile?**

A: The app works on mobile browsers, but the experience is optimized for desktop. Voice input may have limited support on mobile.

---

**Q: How accurate is the filler word detection?**

A: Very accurate for common filler words. It uses pattern matching to detect words like "um", "uh", "like", etc.

---

### Interview Preparation

**Q: Should I memorize answers?**

A: No! Focus on understanding concepts and having examples ready. Natural, conversational answers are better than memorized scripts.

---

**Q: How do I improve my score?**

A:
1. Be specific with examples
2. Structure your answers (STAR method)
3. Reduce filler words
4. Speak confidently
5. Provide measurable results

---

**Q: What's a good average score?**

A:
- 8-10: Excellent, interview-ready
- 6-7: Good, needs minor refinement
- 4-5: Moderate, needs practice
- Below 4: Needs significant improvement

---

**Q: How do I prepare for challenging mode?**

A:
1. Master friendly and technical modes first
2. Research common interview pitfalls
3. Prepare detailed examples
4. Practice handling criticism
5. Focus on specificity and depth

---

### Resume Analysis

**Q: What format should my resume be?**

A: PDF format only. Ensure text is selectable (not a scanned image).

---

**Q: Is my resume data stored?**

A: No, resume text is processed in-memory and not permanently stored.

---

**Q: Can I upload multiple resumes?**

A: Yes, you can upload different versions and compare the generated questions.

---

**Q: Should I take the roast seriously?**

A: Take the constructive points seriously, but remember it's designed to be humorous. Use it as motivation to improve!
